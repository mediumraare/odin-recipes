Chicken Fricassee

Chicken Fricassee

Classic French dish where chicken pieces are braised in a white wine sauce, with added cream and mushrooms. It's a delightful and creamy chicken dish with a rich sauce.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 celery stalk, sliced
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Season the chicken thighs generously with salt and black pepper.
  2. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs, skin-side down, and cook until they are well-browned on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat evenly. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
  5. Gradually pour in the chicken broth and dry white wine, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  6. Return the browned chicken thighs to the skillet. Add the bay leaf and dried thyme. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  8. Remove the chicken thighs from the skillet and set them aside.
  9. Stir in the heavy cream into the sauce in the skillet. Continue to cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and is heated through.
  10. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, as needed.
  11. Return the chicken thighs to the skillet and warm them in the sauce for a few minutes.
  12. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired.
  13. Serve the chicken fricassee hot, spooning the creamy sauce over the chicken.
